The Human Asteroid.

4 Billion Years Ago

It is hypothesized that several billion years ago earth was nothing but a piece of floating lava only to be hit by an asteroid that carried water, leading it to be cooled down into the planet we all know and love. It is believed to be the sole cause for life on Our Giant Blue Rock.

400 Million Years Ago

After being cooled down, Earth was found to be 3/4th submerged under water with its life consisting of marine life.
Life was flourishing at the time until the planet was struck by its first known Mass Extinction - The Ordovicianâ€“Silurian Mass Extinction, caused by a glaciation attributed to changing sea-levels, pushing upto 85% of marine species towards extinction.

200 Million Years Ago

With life having recovered from the devastating End Permian Mass Extinction, land animals had taken over the rest of the planet with Dinosaurs stomping around and pterosaurs soaring the skies.
It seemed that life could continue flourishing until an asteroid struck Earth wiping out most of all land animals and eliminating upto 18% of all marine genera. This was the Triassic-Jussaric Mass Extinction Event.

80 Million Year Ago

The recent discovery of the Chicxulub Crater near Mexico has proven the theory of yet another mass-extinction as true.
It was theorized that yet another cosmic body had made contact with ours only to wipe out yet another large percentage of life leading to
an impact winter, freezing marine life and bringing a halt to photosynthesis, killing off all the phytoplanktons. This is the most recent mass extinction event called the Cretaceous-Paleogene Mass Extinction.

100,000 Years Ago

With our early ancestors being hunter-gatherers, life flourished as mammals ruled the planet with 70% of all animal life consisting of mammals.
This is the time when fire was discovered which caused to a domino-effect leading to humans getting intelligent.
Our superior intelligence gave us an advantage over most of the species which may have been otherwise physically stronger.

1970

Unlike every other species alive or dead, us humans never chose to adapt to the planet. Instead, we made the planet adapt to us. We invented refrigerators to keep our food and drinks cold during summers, developed heaters to keep us warm during the winters.
With skyscrapers touching clouds and greed-taking over our minds, certain things ended up getting neglected. One of those being harmful emission which gave rise to the sea levels due to global warming.
While the previously mentioned mass-extinction events led to a huge loss of life, it was never at a rate of extinction as constant as it is today. The only difference is that this time around;

2100

It is said that if our ways of operating are not mended, then in a century or two, we will need further adaptations to continue living on what we call our home planet.
Alternatively, we could also branch out onto colonizing other planets as is suggested by many famous minds these days. Nobody knows what the future has in store for us, but climate change is one thing we should all be aware about.
With rising sea levels, there are islands like in Maldives and West of Africa that are at a risk of being submerged by 2030.
These threats are very serious and might lead to further severe calamities such as hurricanes and tornadoes.
